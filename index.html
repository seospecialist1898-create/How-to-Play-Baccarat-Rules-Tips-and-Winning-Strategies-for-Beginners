Understanding Baccarat Rules

The core of any Baccarat casino game is straightforward. The game uses standard decks of Baccarat cards (usually 6-8 decks shuffled together). The goal is to bet on which hand—Player or Banker—will have a total closest to 9. You can also bet on a Tie if you think both hands will be equal.

Card Values in Baccarat:

  • Aces are worth 1 point.
  • Cards 2 through 9 are worth their face value.
  • 10s, Jacks, Queens, and Kings are worth 0 points.

If the total exceeds 9, you subtract 10 (or drop the first digit). For example, a 7 and 8 total 15, which becomes 5.

Betting Options:

  • Player Bet: Pays 1:1 if the Player hand wins.
  • Banker Bet: Pays 1:1 minus a 5% commission (house edge makes this favorable).
  • Tie Bet: Pays 8:1 or 9:1, but it's riskier.

In a typical round of Baccarat online casino play, the dealer deals two cards to the Player and two to the Banker. Depending on the totals, a third card might be drawn based on specific rules:

  • If either hand totals 8 or 9 (a "natural"), no more cards are drawn.
  • Player draws a third card if total is 0-5; stands on 6-7.
  • Banker's third card depends on the Player's third card and Banker's total.

Winning Strategies for Baccarat

While Baccarat is luck-based, some strategies can improve your odds over time:

  • Martingale System: Double your bet after a loss, return to base after a win. Risky for long losing streaks.
  • 1-3-2-4 System: Bet in units following this pattern to minimize losses.
  • Trend Betting: Track patterns like Player or Banker streaks, but remember each hand is independent.
  • Flat Betting: Keep bets consistent to prolong playtime.
